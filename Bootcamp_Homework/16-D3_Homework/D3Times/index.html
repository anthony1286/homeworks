Healthcare and Poverty in the United States

Correlations Discovered Between % of People Lacking Healhcare and % of People in Poverty

The scatter plot above shows each of the 50 states of the USA and where they fall when plotted against the percent of people in poverty (for that state), as well as the percent of people Lacking healthcare.

There is clearly a correlation between people lacking healthcare and people in poverty. A state like Maryland or Connecticut (plotted on the lower left) has a relatively small percentage of residents without healthcare and also a relatively small percentage of residents living in poverty.

This scatter plot identifies a correlation between these two factors. This does not mean that one directly causes the other, but it gives us more information on where to continue to look for more information.
